I am Nathan Hubner

And I'm an Adventurer

About Me

My name is Nathan Hubner. Whether in the mountains or on the sea, I love adventure. Throughout my life I have been drawn to things that seem out of my reach. This is what really made me interested in developing. I have been using computers for a long time, and have always been intrigued at how websites work. As I have learned and grown, I have found a love for creating my own website and my own code.

History

Where I came from...

December 16th, 1995 was a day that went down in history for my family. This is the day that I was born. It all started at a hospital in downtown Salt Lake City. From there I mainly grew up around West Valley City. While living there I was able to attend the same schools that my parents did. It was always interesting seeing teachers remember who my parents were. I found that they often had stories to share.

Where I've been...

As I said before, I have have always been drawn to the call of adventure. From Seattle to St Louis, and from Illinois to Mexico. I have always loved getting out and meeting new people while exploring new cultures at the same time. Most of the time I was just visting for vacation, but I had the opportuinty to do 2 years of service in various parts of Missouri and Illinois. I loved getting to know the individuals and the culture there.

Where I plan on going...

Throughout the next 5 years I have a few places I would like to see myself. I would love to see my skills in developing reach a point where I can start my own company and help people create the websites and apps they would like to make. I would also like to se myself married with a family. As I have recently started developing I have thought heavily on what it will do for me and my family. I mostly it will show them that you can take anything you dream of doing and make it a reality.

Acheivements

The various acheivements I have in life range from acedemic to extra curricular, trivial to triumphant. I think the biggest achievement would have to be the fact that I have never given up on myself. In fact I have always been willing to push myself. Whether it was summiting a 10,000 ft peak, or survivng a slow day at work. I have always been willing to do what it takes to ensure I come off victorious.
